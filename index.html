Paul Jiang

I am a senior at Purdue University pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science with a minor in Mathematics.

My research interests lie in the belief that while the digitization of knowledge defined the last half century, the formalization of knowledge will define the next. The internet made information universally accessible. I aim to make it verifiable. My objective is to bridge the gap between the rigorous reasoning of automated verification and the adaptability of modern AI.

Currently, I am working with Prof. Ganesh Gopalakrishnan at the University of Utah on formalizing the semantics of NVIDIA Tensor Cores and correcting them post-hoc for high precision computation. Simultaneously, I am also working with Prof. Jingbo Wang at Purdue University on certifying the safety of pruned Vision Transformers using differential Zonotope-based abstract interpretation.

I am also seeking Ph.D. programs! :)
